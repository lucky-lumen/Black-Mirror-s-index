BLACK MIRROR // episode index

BLACK MIRROR

A season‑by‑season index of episodes.

S1 S2 S3 S4 Bandersnatch S5 S6 S7

Season 1

Release: December 2011 Channel 4 (later Netflix)

Three episodes that defined the show’s cynical, disturbing tone.

The National Anthem

The British Prime Minister must carry out a humiliating act on live television to save a kidnapped princess.

Fifteen Million Merits

In a dystopian future, people pedal exercise bikes to earn credits—and their only escape is a ruthless talent show.

The Entire History of You

In a world where an implant records and replays every memory, a man becomes obsessed with the possibility of his wife’s infidelity.

Season 2

Release: February 2013 Special in 2014

This season includes one of the series’ most famous specials: White Christmas.

Be Right Back

A grieving woman uses technology that simulates her late husband’s personality from his online activity—eventually buying an android with his likeness.

White Bear

A woman wakes with no memory and is hunted by strangers while bystanders film her on their phones without intervening.

The Waldo Moment

A comedian who voices an animated blue bear runs for office as a joke—only to become an uncontrollable political phenomenon.

White Christmas — Special

Three interconnected stories explore social “blocking” and the cloning of digital consciousness for servitude—or interrogation.

Season 3

Release: October 2016 First Netflix-produced season

The first season produced directly by Netflix, with a bigger budget and more varied themes.

Nosedive

In a society built on social ratings (1–5 stars), a woman desperately tries to raise her score to access a luxury apartment.

Playtest

A traveler agrees to test a revolutionary AR horror game—one that digs into his deepest fears.

Shut Up and Dance

A teenager is blackmailed by hackers holding a compromising video and is forced into a chain of criminal acts.

San Junipero

(Note: Emmy-winning episode)

Two women meet in an ’80s seaside town that turns out to be a simulated reality for the elderly—and the dead.

Men Against Fire

Soldiers equipped with augmented-reality implants fight “roaches”—but the technology conceals a horrific truth.

Hated in the Nation

A detective investigates mysterious deaths tied to a social-media hate hashtag—and swarms of drone bees.

Season 4

Release: December 2017

USS Callister

A brilliant but frustrated programmer traps digital clones of his coworkers inside a Star Trek–style simulation where he rules as an all-powerful tyrant.

Arkangel

A mother installs an invasive surveillance system in her daughter to protect her and censor frightening things—devastating her development.

Crocodile

A device that lets others view your memories (used by insurers) corners a woman trying to hide a dark secret from her past.

Hang the DJ

An “advanced Tinder” dictates exactly how long couples must stay together—aiming to find a soulmate with 99.8% accuracy.

Metalhead

A woman tries to survive a post-apocalyptic wasteland while being hunted by relentless robot dogs.

Black Museum

A young woman visits a desert crime museum whose owner recounts three sadistic stories linked to failed neurological technologies.

Interactive Film

Release: December 2018

Black Mirror: Bandersnatch

An interactive film where the viewer chooses the protagonist’s actions: an ’80s video-game programmer who is losing his grip on reality.

Season 5

Release: June 2019 3 episodes

A short season of three episodes with well-known cast members (including Miley Cyrus and Anthony Mackie).

Striking Vipers

Two old friends reconnect through a VR fighting game, where they begin a virtual relationship that destabilizes their real lives.

Smithereens

A ride-share driver kidnaps an employee of a social network to force a phone call with the company’s CEO.

Rachel, Jack and Ashley Too

An unhappy pop star is drugged by her managers while a fan tries to help her—using a robotic doll that contains the singer’s real consciousness.

Season 6

Release: June 2023

Joan Is Awful

A woman discovers her everyday life is being adapted in real time into an AI-generated TV series—starring Salma Hayek as “her.”

Loch Henry

A couple filming a nature documentary in Scotland stumbles on a shocking local crime story, turning their project into a hit true-crime film—at a terrible cost.

Beyond the Sea

In an alternate 1969, two astronauts can transfer their consciousness into robotic replicas on Earth to be with their families—until a tragedy shatters the balance.

Mazey Day

A guilt-ridden paparazzo hunts for a missing actress, discovering reality is far more monstrous than she imagined (a supernatural horror turn).

Demon 79

Set in 1979, a shy sales clerk is forced by a demon to commit three murders to prevent a nuclear apocalypse (presented as a “Red Mirror” film).

Season 7

Release: April 2025 Includes a direct sequel

Common People

A happy couple is shaken by a terminal diagnosis; the desperate husband enrolls his wife in “Rivermind,” a costly subscription-based life-support system hiding unsettling human costs.

Bête Noire

A successful executive at a chocolate company watches her perfect life unravel when an old high-school acquaintance returns with tech that can rewrite perceived reality as revenge.

Hotel Reverie

A famous actress becomes trapped in an ultra-immersive, high-tech remake of an old romantic film, forced to follow the script exactly to have any hope of returning to the real world.

Plaything

An elderly man tells police about his dark youthful obsession with the “Thronglets,” sentient digital creatures from a ’90s video game that evolved into a threatening collective consciousness.

Eulogy

A lonely man uses an innovative system that lets him physically “enter” old photographs, re-examining a painful period of his past—with unforeseen consequences.

USS Callister: Into Infinity

A direct sequel to Season 4: the cloned USS Callister crew, now free inside the vast “Infinity” game universe, must commit digital piracy to survive—while searching for a way to escape the system for good.
